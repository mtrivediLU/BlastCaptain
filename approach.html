Our Approach

Innovation & Creativity

What We Did:

ChatGPT/Generative AI: We integrated ChatGPT to provide intelligent, conversational analysis of blast data, enabling quick Q&A on blast metrics and summarizing key findings in plain language.

Python Scripts: Python was utilized for data parsing, transformations, and to generate advanced analytics on fragmentation quality, muck pile formation, and other blast parameters.

What We Could Do Next:

LangChain: By chaining AI models and context modules, we could unify multiple tasks (e.g., retrieving data from logs, summarizing entire patterns, forecasting potential hazards) into a seamless workflow.

Microsoft Power Automate & Power Automate Desktop Flow: Automating data ingestion, notifications, or even on-prem tasks via desktop automation scripts (e.g., automatically generating blasts reports, emailing relevant stakeholders) would reduce manual overhead.

ELT Model for a Centralized Data Warehouse: Migrating or structuring raw blasting data into a scalable warehouse first, then applying transformations “at rest,” gives more agility in how the data is processed and consumed across the entire solution.

Why It’s Innovative:

By blending AI-driven insights (ChatGPT) with robust Python-based analytics and the potential for next-level orchestration (LangChain, Power Automate, ELT), the solution rethinks how blasting operations are monitored, optimized, and reported—offering a uniquely creative, data-centric approach.

Technical Feasibility

What We Did:

Python Scripts for ELT: We already move and transform data with Python-based scripts, ensuring raw logs, metrics, and other inputs get cleaned, normalized, and ready for analysis.

What We Could Do Next:

Cloud Deployment (Azure or AWS): Automate these Python processes in the cloud and connect them to a centralized database, ensuring real-time or scheduled data flows for continuous analysis.

UI Integration: The resulting data pipeline feeds directly into the user interface that showcases videos, charts, and analytics—making the entire solution user-friendly for site engineers and managers.

Contextual Knowledge Customization: If further accepted, we can embed domain-specific knowledge (like advanced fragmentation models or blast physics) to refine the logic and deliver even more accurate insights.

Why It’s Feasible:

All Python scripts, cloud services, and database integrations are standard enterprise-ready technologies. The system’s design—pipeline + database + UI—ensures minimal friction to deploy and maintain in real mining environments.

Impact & Relevance

What We Did:

Provided blasting data analysis (fragmentation, dust, MuckPile scores, etc.) in a single, user-friendly dashboard accessible to technical and non-technical stakeholders.

What We Could Do Next:

Automated Alerts & Workflows: If “Environmental_Score” is low or “Fragmentation_Score” is below a threshold, Power Automate could trigger real-time alerts to supervisors or schedule further inspections.

Customizable Logic: Incorporating contextual knowledge from domain experts would further refine analyses—e.g., specialized rules for different rock types, weather conditions, or advanced safety thresholds.

Why It’s Impactful:

Providing detailed, centralized blast insights not only boosts safety and efficiency but accelerates decision-making. By allowing further customization (automations, domain logic), the solution remains highly relevant to ongoing open-pit mining challenges.

Scalability & Sustainability

What We Did:

Designed a modular approach: Python scripts are easily deployed on different infrastructures, and the UI can scale horizontally if more users or blasts are added.

What We Could Do Next:

Centralized Data Warehouse: Transitioning to an ELT paradigm in Azure or AWS ensures more robust growth. As data volumes rise, we can scale storage and compute without re-architecting.

Long-Term Maintainability: Leveraging widely supported services (Microsoft Power Automate, mainstream clouds, Python’s ecosystem) ensures the solution remains cost-effective and future-proof.

Why It’s Sustainable:

All components are enterprise-friendly and can be iteratively extended to cover more mines, new blast patterns, or additional operational data. This modular design and standard tooling guarantee a long lifecycle and wide adoption potential for the solution.
